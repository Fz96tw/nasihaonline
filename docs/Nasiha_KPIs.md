# Nasiha — KPIs & Success Metrics
*Draft — May 2026*

---

## Purpose

This document defines how Nasiha measures whether it is fulfilling its mission. Metrics are organized into four areas: Community Health, Knowledge Exchange Activity, Impact, and Organizational Health. Review cadence and membership targets will be determined by the Board of Directors once the Organization is operational.

---

## 1. Community Health

These metrics tell us whether the community is growing, retaining members, and reflecting the diversity of the global medical field.

| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| Total active members | Members who have contributed or participated at least once in the past quarter |
| Member retention rate | Percentage of members who remain active year over year |
| Geographic diversity | Number of countries represented in the membership |
| New members per quarter | Rate of membership growth over time |
| Student & trainee representation | Proportion of members who are students or early-career trainees |

---

## 2. Knowledge Exchange Activity

These metrics tell us whether the core mission — learning and teaching — is actually happening.

| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| Lectures & webinars per month | Total knowledge-sharing sessions delivered |
| Case discussions per month | Active peer review and consultation sessions |
| Total credits earned | Cumulative contribution volume across the community |
| Total credits spent | Cumulative demand for expertise across the community |
| Credit exchange ratio | Balance between giving and taking — a healthy ratio means the exchange is sustainable |
| Unique contributor-recipient pairings | Whether exchange is spread across many members or concentrated among a few |
| Research resources shared | Number of curated resources contributed to the community |

---

## 3. Impact

These metrics tell us whether Nasiha is making a real difference — harder to measure, but the most important.

| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| Member satisfaction score | Periodic survey asking members whether Nasiha is genuinely useful to them |
| Self-reported outcomes | Members sharing how a lecture, mentorship, or consultation made a tangible difference |
| Trainee progression rate | How many student/trainee members grow into Active Member status over time |
| Repeat engagement rate | Whether members return to the exchange repeatedly, indicating ongoing value |

---

## 4. Organizational Health

These metrics tell us whether Nasiha is being run well as an organization.

| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| Budget adherence | Whether actual spend tracks to the Board-approved annual budget |
| Board meeting cadence | Whether the Board is meeting at least quarterly as required by the charter |
| Membership application turnaround | Time from application submission to decision |
| Code of conduct incidents | Number of reported violations and resolution outcomes |
| Digital ledger accuracy | Whether credit records are maintained reliably and transparently |

---

## Review Cadence

*To be determined by the Board of Directors once the Organization is operational.*

---

## Membership Targets

*To be set by the Board of Directors once Nasiha has launched and baseline data is available. The Organization will prioritize quality of engagement over growth for its own sake.*

---

## Notes

- Metrics should be reviewed together, not in isolation. A large membership with low exchange activity is less meaningful than a smaller community with high engagement.
- Impact metrics are the hardest to collect but the most important. The Board should invest in simple, regular feedback mechanisms from the earliest stage.
- As the digital ledger matures, exchange activity metrics should become largely automated. Early on, they may require manual tracking.
