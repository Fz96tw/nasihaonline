# Nasiha — Member Communications Framework
*Draft — May 2026*

---

## Philosophy

Communication within Nasiha should feel like a trusted professional community — not a mailing list, not a social media feed, and never a source of unwanted messages. Members join to learn and teach, not to be marketed to or overwhelmed.

Two principles guide everything:

1. **No direct spam.** Members cannot cold-message each other without consent. All unsolicited contact goes through a Nasiha relay — the Organization acts as an intermediary, not a pass-through for personal inboxes.
2. **Community over broadcast.** The default mode of communication is shared discussion, not one-to-many announcements. Members should feel they are part of a conversation, not an audience.

---

## Communication Channels

### 1. Platform Inbox / Relay (Member-to-Member)

When a member wants to reach another member directly — to request a consultation, propose a collaboration, or follow up on an exchange — they do so through the Nasiha platform relay, not by contacting them directly.

**How it works:**
- Member A submits a message or request through the Nasiha system, addressed to Member B.
- The system notifies Member B that they have a message waiting.
- Member B can choose to accept, decline, or respond — all within the platform.
- Personal contact details (email, phone, social media) are never shared without explicit consent from both parties.
- If both members agree to connect directly after an exchange, they may choose to share contact information at that point.

**What this protects against:**
- Cold outreach and spam
- Unwanted solicitation or self-promotion
- Privacy violations or misuse of member contact information

---

### 2. Group Discussion Forums

Topic-based discussion spaces where members can post questions, share resources, discuss cases, and engage with the broader community. Forums are the primary space for asynchronous, community-wide interaction.

**Suggested forum categories:**

| Forum | Purpose |
|---|---|
| General | Community announcements, introductions, open discussion |
| Clinical Discussions | Case-based learning, diagnostic questions, treatment approaches |
| Research & Resources | Sharing articles, tools, guidelines, and curated learning materials |
| Teaching & Mentorship | Advice on teaching, mentorship requests, pedagogical discussion |
| Students & Trainees | A dedicated space for early-career members to ask questions freely |
| Organizational | Board updates, policy discussions, credit system questions |

**Forum norms:**
- All posts are visible to the full membership — members should write accordingly.
- Commercial promotion, job postings, and fee-based services are not permitted.
- Disagreement is welcome; disrespect is not. Forum moderation falls under the Code of Conduct.
- Members can follow specific forums and receive digests rather than real-time notifications, to avoid inbox overload.

## Organizational Communications

### Announcements
The Board and Officers will issue periodic announcements to the full membership covering organizational updates, upcoming events, new members, and community milestones. Announcements should be infrequent enough to carry weight — members should feel that when Nasiha sends something, it matters.

### Event Notifications
Members will be notified of upcoming lectures, webinars, and case discussions. Notifications should be targeted where possible — members interested in cardiology should not receive notifications about dermatology events unless they opt in.

### Credit Statements
Each member receives a periodic credit statement summarizing their balance, recent transactions, and lifetime contribution total. This serves both as a practical record and as recognition of their contribution.

### The Nasiha Digest
A regular community digest (frequency to be determined) summarizing recent activity: top discussions, new resources shared, upcoming events, and member spotlights. The digest celebrates what the community is doing and helps members stay connected without requiring daily engagement.

## Privacy & Contact Information

- Member contact details are never published in the member directory without explicit consent.
- The member directory shows name, specialty, and general location by default.
- Members may choose to make additional information visible (e.g., institutional affiliation, areas of expertise, willingness to be contacted for specific topics).
- The Organization will never sell, share, or use member data for any purpose outside of running Nasiha.

---

## Platform Selection

The specific platform(s) used to host the relay inbox, discussion forums, and announcements will be determined by the Board of Directors once the Organization is operational. Key criteria for platform selection:

- **Privacy controls:** Ability to manage who can contact whom and how.
- **Accessibility:** Works across devices and geographies without requiring expensive software.
- **Moderation tools:** Ability to enforce the Code of Conduct in forums.
- **Cost:** Free or low-cost tiers available, especially in early phases.
- **Scalability:** Can grow with the membership without a complete rebuild.

Candidate platforms to evaluate include Slack, Discord, Circle, Mighty Networks, and purpose-built community tools. A simple email listserv or WhatsApp structure may suffice for the founding cohort phase before a more formal platform is needed.

---

## Communication Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- **Frequent all-member blasts.** If every week brings a new announcement, members tune out. Reserve broadcast messages for things that genuinely matter.
- **Open direct messaging without consent.** Even well-intentioned messages from strangers feel intrusive. The relay model protects everyone.
- **Unmoderated forums.** Discussion spaces without clear norms and light moderation quickly become low-value or hostile. Assign forum stewards from the early membership.
- **Notification overload.** Give members control over what they hear about and how often. Overwhelming people drives disengagement.
