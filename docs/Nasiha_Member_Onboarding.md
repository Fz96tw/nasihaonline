# Nasiha — Member Onboarding
*Draft — May 2026*

---

## Philosophy

The first few weeks of a member's experience determine whether they become an active contributor or quietly disappear. Onboarding should feel personal, not bureaucratic — a warm welcome into a community that is genuinely glad to have them, followed by a clear path to their first meaningful contribution.

The goal of onboarding is not just orientation. It is activation.

---

## The Onboarding Journey

### Step 1 — Welcome (Day 1)

When a new member is approved, they receive a personal welcome message from a founding member or designated Member Ambassador. This is not an automated email — it is a human note that acknowledges who they are and why they were invited or accepted.

The welcome message includes:
- A warm personal greeting
- A brief summary of what Nasiha is and how it works
- Links to the Charter, Code of Conduct, and key community spaces
- Their starting credit balance (zero) and how to earn their first credits
- The name of their assigned Member Guide (see Step 2)
- An invitation to introduce themselves in the General forum

---

### Step 2 — Member Guide Assignment

Every new member is paired with an existing Member Guide — an active member who volunteers to be a point of contact for the first 60 days. The Member Guide's role is light-touch: check in once or twice, answer questions, and help the new member find their footing.

**Member Guide responsibilities:**
- Send a brief intro message within the first 3 days
- Point the new member toward relevant forums and upcoming events
- Flag any contribution opportunities that match the new member's expertise
- Check in at the 30-day mark

Member Guides earn Nasiha Credits for their time. As the community grows, experienced members should be encouraged to take on Guide responsibilities.

---

### Step 3 — Orientation (Week 1)

New members receive a short orientation sequence — not a lengthy manual, but a few focused pieces of information delivered over the first week:

| Day | Topic |
|---|---|
| Day 1 | Welcome + community overview |
| Day 3 | How Nasiha Credits work |
| Day 5 | How to find and join discussions |
| Day 7 | How to offer your first contribution |

This can be delivered via the platform inbox relay or a simple email sequence, depending on the platform chosen.

---

### Step 4 — First Contribution (Within 30 Days)

The single most important milestone in onboarding is a member's first contribution — their first lecture offer, forum post, case discussion, or resource share. Members who contribute once are far more likely to stay engaged.

The Member Guide and platform should actively nudge new members toward this milestone:
- Highlight open requests that match their expertise
- Suggest a low-barrier entry point (e.g., sharing a useful article in the Research forum)
- Celebrate first contributions publicly in the community digest

---

### Step 5 — 60-Day Check-In

At 60 days, new members receive a brief check-in from the Organization:
- How is the experience going?
- Have they made their first contribution?
- Is there anything confusing or missing?
- Would they like to remain an Associate Member or are they ready to move toward Active status?

This is also an opportunity to collect early feedback on the onboarding process itself.

---

## Member Profile Setup

Upon joining, each member completes a profile that includes:

- Name and title
- Specialty or field
- Career stage (student, trainee, practicing, retired)
- Geographic location (country/region)
- Areas of expertise they are willing to share
- Topics they are most interested in learning
- Communication preferences (digest frequency, notification settings)
- Whether they consent to being listed in the member directory and at what level of detail

Profile completion should be encouraged but not required on Day 1 — give new members a few days to settle in before pushing for completeness.

---

## Student & Trainee Onboarding

Students and trainees follow the same onboarding journey but with adjusted expectations:
- Their first contribution can be lighter (a resource share, a forum question, participation in a case discussion as a learner)
- Their Member Guide should ideally be someone who is a few career stages ahead — a practicing professional who can also serve informally as a mentor
- They are explicitly welcomed into the Students & Trainees forum as their primary home base

---

## Onboarding Checklist (for the Organization)

For each new member, the Organization should confirm:

- [ ] Welcome message sent within 24 hours of approval
- [ ] Member Guide assigned and introduced within 3 days
- [ ] Orientation sequence initiated
- [ ] Profile setup prompted
- [ ] Member listed in the directory (per their consent settings)
- [ ] Credit account created in the digital ledger with zero balance
- [ ] 30-day nudge scheduled
- [ ] 60-day check-in scheduled
