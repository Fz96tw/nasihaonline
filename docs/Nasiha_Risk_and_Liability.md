# Nasiha — Risk & Liability Framework
*Draft — May 2026*

---

## Overview

Nasiha operates at the intersection of medical knowledge, community engagement, and non-profit governance. This creates real risks that the Organization must anticipate and manage — not to become overly cautious or legalistic, but to protect members, the public, and the Organization itself.

This document identifies the key risk areas and establishes principles and policies to address them. The Board of Directors should consult a legal professional before finalizing this framework, particularly given Nasiha's international scope.

---

## 1. Medical Advice & Clinical Liability

**The Risk**
Members sharing clinical knowledge in Nasiha's forums, lectures, or consultations could be interpreted as providing medical advice. If a member or third party acts on information shared in Nasiha and suffers harm, the Organization or individual members could face liability.

**Policies**
- Nasiha is an **educational community**, not a clinical service. This must be stated clearly and consistently across all platforms, communications, and onboarding materials.
- All knowledge shared within Nasiha is for **educational and professional development purposes only** and does not constitute medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment recommendations for specific patients.
- Members must not share identifiable patient information under any circumstances (see also: Privacy & Data Protection).
- When discussing cases, members must use de-identified, anonymized scenarios only.
- Individual members are responsible for applying their own professional judgment when using knowledge gained through Nasiha in clinical settings.

**Standard Disclaimer (to appear on all Nasiha platforms and communications):**
> *Nasiha is an educational knowledge-sharing community. Content shared by members — including lectures, case discussions, consultations, and forum posts — is intended for professional learning purposes only and does not constitute medical advice. Members are responsible for exercising independent clinical judgment in their own practice. Nasiha and its members accept no liability for clinical decisions made based on content shared within the community.*

## 2. Privacy & Data Protection

**The Risk**
Nasiha collects and stores member information and facilitates communication between members internationally. This creates obligations under data protection laws including GDPR (European Union), and equivalent regulations in other jurisdictions.

**Policies**
- Member personal data is collected only for the purpose of running Nasiha and is never sold, shared, or used for any other purpose.
- Members control what information appears in their public profile and can update or remove it at any time.
- Patient data must never be shared in any Nasiha space — forums, relay messages, or live sessions. Violations are treated as serious Code of Conduct breaches.
- The Organization shall adopt a formal Privacy Policy before launching to the public. The Privacy Policy will specify what data is collected, how it is stored, who can access it, and how members can request deletion.
- The digital ledger (credit system) contains transaction records. Members have the right to view their own records. Aggregate data may be used for organizational reporting but individual transaction data is not shared publicly.
- The Board shall appoint a Privacy Lead responsible for maintaining compliance as the Organization grows internationally.

## 3. Member Conduct & Reputational Risk

**The Risk**
A member behaving inappropriately — spreading misinformation, harassing others, or misusing the platform — could harm other members or damage Nasiha's reputation.

**Policies**
- The Code of Conduct (defined in the Charter) governs all member behavior. Violations are handled through the enforcement process: warning for a first violation, suspension or removal for serious or repeated violations.
- The Board has the authority to remove members who pose a risk to the community or the Organization's reputation.
- Nasiha does not verify the clinical accuracy of every piece of content shared by members. Members are expected to share responsibly and within their areas of competence. Other members are encouraged to respectfully flag inaccurate or misleading content via the reporting mechanism.
- Forum moderation should be active enough to catch obvious problems quickly, without suppressing legitimate debate.

---

## 4. Intellectual Property

**The Risk**
Members may share copyrighted materials — journal articles, presentations, third-party content — without proper authorization. This could expose the Organization to copyright infringement claims.

**Policies**
- Members may share links to copyrighted content but should not upload or reproduce full copyrighted texts without appropriate licensing or fair use justification.
- Original content created by members for Nasiha (lectures, resources, forum posts) remains the intellectual property of the member. By sharing it within Nasiha, the member grants the Organization a non-exclusive right to make it available to the membership.
- Nasiha will not commercialize member-created content without explicit written consent.
- The Board shall develop a clear IP policy for member-created content as the Organization grows and a knowledge library is established.

---

## 5. Financial & Governance Risk

**The Risk**
As a non-profit, Nasiha must comply with regulations governing charitable organizations, grant management, and financial reporting. Mismanagement could jeopardize non-profit status or expose the Board to personal liability.

**Policies**
- The Treasurer maintains accurate records and reports to the Board quarterly.
- No single funding source should represent more than 40% of annual revenue.
- The Board reviews and approves all expenditures above a threshold defined in the annual budget.
- Founding members and Board directors should be aware of their fiduciary duties and, if needed, obtain Directors & Officers (D&O) liability insurance as the Organization grows.
- An annual financial summary is shared with the membership for transparency.

---

## 6. International Operations

**The Risk**
Nasiha intends to operate internationally from the outset. Different countries have different rules governing non-profits, medical education, data privacy, and professional conduct.

**Policies**
- Nasiha will be incorporated in a single jurisdiction (to be determined by the Board) and will operate internationally under that legal framework.
- The standard disclaimer and Privacy Policy will be written to meet the most stringent applicable standards (e.g., GDPR-compliant by default).
- The Board should seek legal advice on international operations before launching Phase 2 or 3 membership.
- Where local regulations restrict certain activities (e.g., specific types of clinical discussion), Nasiha will defer to those restrictions for members in those jurisdictions.

## Legal Incorporation Checklist

Nasiha will be incorporated in **Missouri** as a non-stock non-profit corporation. The steps and links below are Missouri-specific.

**Phase 1 — First 30 Days**
- [ ] Search the Missouri Secretary of State business registry to confirm "Nasiha" is available: https://bsd.sos.mo.gov/
- [ ] File Articles of Incorporation (non-stock corporation) with the Missouri Secretary of State via the same portal (~$25)
- [ ] Apply for an Employer Identification Number (EIN) via IRS Form SS-4 (free) at https://sa.www4.irs.gov/modiein/individual/index.jsp — use the official IRS website only, not third-party services
- [ ] Confirm three Board members in the roles of President, Treasurer, and Secretary (Uzma Khan as Founder & Board Member, Nadeem Haider and Nighat Abidi as Board Members)
- [ ] Draft formal Bylaws (the Nasiha Charter serves as the basis — "Bylaws" is the legal term required for incorporation and the 501(c)(3) application)

**Phase 2 — Months 1–3**
- [ ] Establish a business checking account (requires two Board member signatures)
- [ ] Develop board meeting guidelines and draft agenda for first formal Board meeting
- [ ] Research need for general liability insurance
- [ ] Draft organizational budget and fundraising plan
- [ ] Apply for Solicitation License if required in the city where Nasiha does business (~$15 — not required in Columbia, MO for nonprofits)

**Phase 3 — Months 3–6**
- [ ] Submit Federal 501(c)(3) application via IRS Form 1023 at https://www.pay.gov/public/form/start/62759871 (~$600) — requires Articles of Incorporation, EIN, list of three officers, Bylaws, and statement of Revenue and Expenses
- [ ] Submit application for Missouri State Employment Insurance Program at https://labor.mo.gov/employers
- [ ] Apply for Property Tax Exemption with local government if applicable — must be done before sourcing any property
- [ ] Obtain general liability insurance and, if applicable, workers' compensation insurance
- [ ] Host first formal Board meeting: vote on officer roles, approve Bylaws, approve budget

**Phase 4 — Months 6–9**
- [ ] Apply for Missouri State Sales Tax Exemption (free) via Form 1746 at https://dor.mo.gov/forms/1746.pdf
- [ ] Enroll in E-Verify if Nasiha hires any employees (free) at https://www.e-verify.gov/employers/enrolling-in-e-verify
- [ ] Develop policies, procedures, and job descriptions if staff are brought on

**Phase 5 — Months 9–12**
- [ ] Focus on identifying individual donors and increasing grant applications
- [ ] Develop a community relations plan

**Estimated Startup Costs (Year 1):** $900–$1,000 covering all Missouri government filing fees and basic legal consultation. Technology, branding, and operational costs are additional.
