# Nasiha — Content & Knowledge Library
*Draft — May 2026*

---

## Philosophy

Every lecture delivered, every case discussed, every resource shared in Nasiha has the potential to help someone beyond the moment it was created. The Knowledge Library is how Nasiha builds institutional memory — turning individual acts of generosity into a lasting, searchable resource for the whole community.

The library should be easy to contribute to, easy to search, and built to grow over time without becoming overwhelming or disorganized.

---

## What Goes in the Library

The Knowledge Library houses content across three categories, mirroring Nasiha's Research-Review-Teach framework:

### Teach — Original Content
Content created by Nasiha members specifically for the community:
- Recorded lectures and webinars
- Written educational summaries and explainers
- Teaching cases (anonymized and de-identified)
- Slide decks and presentation materials shared by lecturers

### Research — Curated Resources
External resources that members have found valuable and curated for the community:
- Links to journal articles, systematic reviews, and clinical guidelines
- Annotated reading lists by specialty or topic
- Recommended textbooks, podcasts, and online courses
- Summaries of key findings with member commentary

### Review — Peer Discussion Archives
Records of community knowledge exchange that are worth preserving:
- Summaries of significant case discussions (anonymized)
- Forum threads that generated particularly valuable insight
- Q&A archives from webinars and live sessions

## Organization & Taxonomy

Content in the library is organized by:

**Specialty / Field**
e.g., Cardiology, Oncology, Primary Care, Public Health, Medical Education, Research Methods

**Content Type**
e.g., Lecture Recording, Case Study, Guideline Summary, Reading List, Forum Archive

**Career Stage**
e.g., Student-Friendly, Early Career, Advanced, All Levels

**Date Added**
So members can find recent contributions easily

**Contributor**
Members can browse content by contributor, which also serves as a visible record of contribution — part of Nasiha's recognition model.

## Contributing to the Library

Any member can contribute to the library. Contributions earn Nasiha Credits (see Credit System):

| Contribution Type | Process |
|---|---|
| Lecture or webinar recording | Submitted after session; reviewed by a library steward before publishing |
| Written explainer or summary | Submitted via a standard template; light editorial review |
| Curated external resource | Submitted with a brief annotation explaining why it is valuable |
| Teaching case | Submitted with confirmation that all patient information is de-identified |
| Forum thread nomination | Any member can nominate a forum thread for archiving |

All contributions go through a brief review by a **Library Steward** — a volunteer member responsible for checking quality, tagging content correctly, and confirming the medical disclaimer is in place before publishing.

---

## Quality & Accuracy

Nasiha does not formally peer-review all library content — that would create a bottleneck incompatible with the community model. Instead, quality is maintained through:

- **Contributor accountability:** Members contribute under their name and are expected to share within their areas of competence (Code of Conduct).
- **Community flagging:** Any member can flag content they believe is inaccurate or outdated. Flagged content is reviewed by a Library Steward and, if needed, the Board.
- **Date visibility:** All content shows when it was added. Members should treat older content with appropriate caution, especially in fast-moving clinical areas.
- **Standard disclaimer:** All library content carries the Nasiha educational disclaimer — it is for learning purposes only and does not constitute clinical advice.

---

## Access

- The Knowledge Library is accessible to all members regardless of credit balance.
- Content is not publicly accessible — it is a member benefit, not a public resource (at least in early phases).
- The Board may choose to make select content publicly available in future phases to demonstrate Nasiha's value and attract new members.
- Members can bookmark, download (where permitted), and share library links within the community.

---

## Library Stewards

Library Stewards are volunteer members who maintain the library's quality and organization. Responsibilities include:

- Reviewing submitted content before it is published
- Tagging and categorizing content correctly
- Responding to flagged content
- Periodically reviewing older content for continued relevance
- Suggesting gaps in the library that could be filled by community contributions

Stewards earn Nasiha Credits for their time. Each specialty area should ideally have a designated Steward once the library reaches sufficient scale.

---

## Growth Strategy

**Phase 1 — Founding Cohort**
The library starts small — a curated collection of resources that founding members share to seed the content. Focus on quality over quantity. A library with 20 excellent resources is more valuable than one with 200 mediocre ones.

**Phase 2 — Referral Growth**
As membership grows, the library grows with it. Introduce the formal contribution and stewardship process. Begin organizing by specialty.

**Phase 3 — Scale**
The library becomes a significant organizational asset. Consider searchability improvements, a featured content section, and eventually a public-facing highlights section to showcase Nasiha's impact.

---

## Technology

In Phase 1, the library can live in a well-organized shared folder (Google Drive or Notion) with a simple index document. As the community scales, it should migrate to the community platform's native content management features, or a dedicated library tool integrated with the broader Nasiha platform (see Technology Roadmap).

Minimum requirements for the library tool:
- Search by keyword, specialty, and content type
- Clear contributor attribution
- Date stamps on all content
- Flag/report mechanism for inaccurate content
- Access control (members only)
