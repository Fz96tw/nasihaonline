# Nasiha — Organizational Charter & Mission Statement
*A Non-Profit Organization Serving the Medical Community*
*Draft — May 2026*

---

## Dedication

*Nasiha is dedicated to Narjis and Syed Iftikhar Hussain Abidi, who guided us toward a life of learning.*

---

## Preamble

We, the founding members of Nasiha, establish this organization in the belief that knowledge is a shared resource — not a commodity. In the medical field, access to expertise, training, and professional guidance can be the difference between lives saved and opportunities lost. Yet such access is too often limited by geography, finances, and professional siloes.

Nasiha — meaning *sincere advice and guidance* — is founded to change that. We are dedicated to spreading knowledge by encouraging each one of us to learn and to teach. We envision a global community of medical professionals, educators, students, and advocates who come together to exchange knowledge freely, support one another generously, and build a more equitable healthcare ecosystem.

This charter establishes the principles, structure, and commitments that will guide Nasiha as it grows.

---

## Mission Statement

> *Nasiha is dedicated to spreading knowledge by encouraging each one of us to learn and to teach. We exist to create a community of learners focused on knowledge sharing — one that acknowledges the willingness of those around us who help us learn, and that shares knowledge unconditionally with others.*

### What We Do

**Research** — We find and curate resources that help our members learn, stay current, and deepen their expertise.

**Review** — We guide each other to improve, offering honest and constructive feedback rooted in shared commitment to growth.

**Teach** — We share our knowledge unconditionally with those around us, because every person who learns becomes a person who can teach.

---

## Vision

A world in which every medical professional — regardless of where they practice, what they can afford, or where they are in their career — has access to a trusted network of peers, mentors, and experts committed to the advancement of their craft and the wellbeing of patients everywhere.

---

## Core Values

**1. Knowledge as a Common Good**
We believe medical expertise should be shared, not hoarded. No lecture, consultation, or mentorship within Nasiha carries a fee. Every exchange is a gift to the community.

**2. Reciprocity**
We operate on a pay-it-forward model. Those who receive guidance are expected to contribute in kind — through lectures, mentorship, or other forms of service — sustaining a cycle of giving that benefits the whole community.

**3. Inclusion & Accessibility**
Nasiha welcomes members from all corners of the world, all career stages, and all medical disciplines. We are especially committed to serving those in under-resourced environments where professional support is hardest to come by.

**4. Trust & Integrity**
Our community depends on honest, evidence-based exchange. Members are expected to share knowledge responsibly, disclose the limits of their expertise, and maintain the highest standards of professional ethics.

**5. Humility & Lifelong Learning**
No one in Nasiha is only a teacher. Every expert is also a student. We cultivate a culture where curiosity is celebrated, questions are welcomed, and growth never stops.

## Article I — Name & Legal Status

The organization shall be known as **Nasiha** (the "Organization"). Nasiha shall be organized and operated as a non-profit organization in accordance with applicable law. The Organization shall not distribute earnings or profits to its members, directors, or officers. All resources will be directed toward advancing the Organization's mission.

*Note: This Charter serves as the governing document of the Organization and the basis for formal Bylaws required for legal incorporation and 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status application. The Board of Directors shall adopt formal Bylaws consistent with this Charter upon incorporation.*

---

## Article II — Purpose

Nasiha is organized exclusively for educational and charitable purposes, including:

- Facilitating the free exchange of medical knowledge through lectures, seminars, case discussions, mentorship, and peer consultation.
- Operating a knowledge-exchange system ("Nasiha Credits") that tracks and rewards member contributions, enabling equitable access to expertise across the community.
- Building a curated directory of medical experts and skilled professionals available for consultation, collaboration, and community engagement.
- Supporting financially constrained members and organizations through subsidized access to training, education, and professional guidance.
- Promoting international collaboration among medical professionals, researchers, educators, and students.

## Article III — Membership

### Eligibility

Membership in Nasiha is open internationally to individuals who are engaged in or studying the medical, healthcare, or public health fields and who share the Organization's values of open knowledge exchange and community contribution. Eligible categories include:

- Practicing physicians and medical specialists
- Retired medical professionals
- Medical students, residents, and trainees
- Allied health professionals (including nurses, pharmacists, therapists, and public health workers)

### Admission — Phased Approach

Nasiha's membership will grow in deliberate phases to ensure quality, trust, and alignment with the mission:

**Phase 1 — Founding Cohort:** The Organization begins with an invite-only cohort selected personally by the founding members. This seeding group establishes the culture and community standards before broader admission opens.

**Phase 2 — Referral-Driven Growth:** Existing members may refer prospective applicants. Referred applicants still complete a standard application, but the referral serves as a community voucher. This allows the network to grow while preserving trust.

**Phase 3 — Open Applications:** Once the community is established, applications will be accepted openly. All applicants must submit credentials, a brief statement of intent, and at least one professional reference. Applications are reviewed and approved by the Board or a designated Membership Committee.

At all phases, the Board of Directors retains the right to review and approve or decline any application.

### Membership Tiers

**Active Members** are those who contribute regularly to the community through teaching, reviewing, or research activities. Active Members have full access to the Nasiha exchange and are eligible to vote in organizational matters.

**Associate Members** are newer members or those in a period of lower activity who are still building their footing in the community. Associate Members have full access to the exchange and are expected to grow toward Active status over time.

**Student & Trainee Members** are a sub-category of Associate Members. Recognizing the demands of medical training, students and trainees have lighter contribution requirements. They are encouraged to contribute in ways suited to their stage — case discussions, research support, peer learning — without the same expectations placed on practicing professionals.

### Inactivity

Members who become inactive will be contacted by the Organization with a friendly nudge to re-engage. No member will be removed solely for inactivity. The Board may establish specific inactivity thresholds and re-engagement procedures as the community grows.

### Application & Due Diligence

The Board of Directors shall establish and maintain due diligence procedures for all applicants, which may include credential verification, professional references, and background review. The goal is to ensure that all members are who they say they are and share Nasiha's values — not to create barriers to participation.

### Nasiha Credits

The Organization shall operate a credit-based exchange system to recognize and reward member contributions, and to facilitate equitable access to expertise across the community.

**Guiding Principle**
Credits are a tool for recognition and reciprocity — not gatekeeping. All members retain full access to the Nasiha community and its knowledge exchange regardless of their credit balance. A zero or negative balance does not restrict participation.

**Unit of Value**
One credit equals one hour of contribution. All contributions are valued equally regardless of the member's seniority, specialty, or background. A retired physician's lecture and a student's research support are worth the same per hour.

**Earning Credits**

| Activity | Credits Earned |
|---|---|
| Delivering a lecture or webinar | 1 per hour |
| One-on-one mentorship session | 1 per hour |
| Participating in a case discussion (as expert) | 0.5 per session |
| Curating and sharing a research resource | 0.5 per resource |
| Administrative or volunteer work | 1 per hour |

**Spending Credits**

| Activity | Credits Required |
|---|---|
| Requesting a one-on-one consultation | 1 per hour |
| Requesting mentorship | 1 per session |
| Commissioning a focused research resource | 0.5 per resource |
| Attending lectures or webinars | Free — always open to all members |

**Credit Limits**
Credits within Nasiha:
- Cannot be converted to cash or any monetary value
- Cannot be used to influence governance, votes, or Board decisions
- Do not grant priority access or queue-jumping rights over other members
- Cannot be transferred, gifted, or sold between members

**Expiry**
Credits do not expire. The Board of Directors shall review this policy annually and may introduce expiry rules if the exchange becomes significantly imbalanced over time.

**Digital Ledger**
The Organization shall maintain a digital ledger to ensure reliable and transparent tracking of all credit transactions. For each transaction, the ledger shall record the activity type, date, members involved, and credits exchanged. Each member shall have visibility into their own balance and transaction history. The ledger shall also track each member's lifetime contribution total, providing lasting recognition for long-term contributors independent of their current balance. The Board shall determine the appropriate platform for the ledger as the Organization grows, starting with a simple shared system and evolving toward a dedicated tool as membership scales.

### Code of Conduct

All members agree to uphold the following principles as a condition of membership:

1. **Share honestly.** Only teach within your areas of competence. Be clear about the limits of your knowledge.
2. **Give generously.** Contribute without expectation of immediate return. Trust that the community will reciprocate.
3. **Receive graciously.** Acknowledge and credit those who help you. Recognize that someone gave their time.
4. **Engage respectfully.** Disagree with ideas, not people. Critique work constructively and with kindness.
5. **Protect privacy.** Never share patient information or personal member data outside the community.
6. **Uphold the mission.** Do not use Nasiha for commercial gain, self-promotion, or any purpose at odds with free knowledge sharing.
7. **Report concerns.** If you witness behavior that violates these principles, bring it to the Board. The health of the community depends on everyone looking out for it.

**Enforcement:** A first violation of the Code of Conduct will result in a formal warning from the Board. A second violation, or a single serious violation, may result in suspension or removal from the Organization, at the Board's discretion.

## Article IV — Governance

**Board of Directors**
Nasiha shall be governed by a Board of Directors consisting of no fewer than three (3) members. The Board is responsible for strategic direction, financial oversight, policy decisions, and ensuring the Organization remains true to its mission. Uzma Khan (Founder) together with Board Members Nadeem Haider and Nighat Abidi shall serve as the inaugural Board of Directors.

**Officers**
The Board shall appoint Officers including, at minimum, a President, a Secretary, and a Treasurer. Officers shall serve in their roles as determined by the Board and may be compensated for extraordinary service as the Organization grows, subject to Board approval.

**Decision Making**
The Board shall operate by consensus where possible. Formal decisions shall require a majority vote of directors. The Board shall meet no less than quarterly, and special meetings may be called by any director.

---

## Article V — Funding & Finances

Nasiha may receive funding through grants, donations, institutional partnerships, and other lawful means consistent with its non-profit status. The Organization shall maintain transparent financial records and operate within an annual budget approved by the Board. No fees shall be charged to members for knowledge exchange activities. The Board may authorize nominal administrative fees for specific programs where necessary to cover costs.

---

## Article VI — Amendments

This charter may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the Board of Directors, provided that proposed amendments have been circulated to all directors at least fourteen (14) days prior to the vote. Amendments shall not alter the core mission or non-profit purpose of the Organization.

---

## Article VII — Dissolution

In the event of dissolution, all remaining assets of the Organization shall be distributed to one or more non-profit organizations with a similar mission, as determined by the Board of Directors in accordance with applicable law. No assets shall be distributed to members, directors, or officers upon dissolution.
